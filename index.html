About Me

I'm a third-year student from the Computer Science and Technology department at ShanghaiTech University, currently participating in the Visiting International Student Program (VISP) at University of Wisconsin-Madison. I'm passionate about data science, computer vision, and financial modeling, with a particular interest in how these technologies can improve society and business efficiency.

My academic journey has equipped me with a solid foundation in computer science while nurturing my curiosity for data-driven research. As part of my coursework, I've completed projects in predicting Weibo user interactions and developing methods for quadratic programming optimization. I enjoy the process of collecting data from different sources and applying computational techniques to uncover patterns that aren't easily observable.

Through my research experiences at the Mobile Perception Lab and Knowledge Computing Laboratory, I've developed skills in dataset creation, algorithm development, and model fine-tuning. I'm particularly drawn to applications that have tangible benefits, such as financial data analysis and object pose estimation.
